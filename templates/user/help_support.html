Getting Started

This section provides an overview of the CyberSentry platform and guides you through the initial setup process.

Platform Overview

CyberSentry is an advanced threat analysis platform designed to help security professionals identify, analyze, and respond to cyber threats efficiently. The platform combines real-time threat intelligence, machine learning, and expert analysis to provide comprehensive security insights.

System Requirements

  • Modern web browser (Chrome 80+, Firefox 75+, Edge 85+, Safari 14+)
  • Internet connection with minimum 10 Mbps download speed
  • 4GB RAM minimum (8GB recommended)
  • Screen resolution of 1366x768 or higher

Account Setup

  1. Navigate to the login page and click "Create Account"
  2. Enter your organization details and contact information
  3. Select your subscription plan or enter your license key
  4. Set up multi-factor authentication (strongly recommended)
  5. Configure your notification preferences

Error Codes

  • Error 1001: Authentication failure. Check your API credentials.
  • Error 1002: Rate limit exceeded. Reduce the frequency of your API calls.

Frequently Asked Questions

How frequently is the threat intelligence database updated?

Our threat intelligence database is updated in real-time with feeds from multiple sources. Critical threats are propagated immediately, while routine updates occur every 15 minutes.

Can I integrate CyberSentry with my existing SIEM solution?

Yes, CyberSentry offers integration with major SIEM platforms including Splunk, IBM QRadar, and Microsoft Sentinel. You can use our API or pre-built connectors available in your SIEM marketplace.

How does the scoring system for threat severity work?

Our threat severity scoring uses a combination of the CVSS (Common Vulnerability Scoring System) and our proprietary impact assessment algorithm. The score ranges from 1-10, with 10 being the most severe. Factors considered include exploit availability, potential impact, and relevance to your specific environment.

What data retention policies are in place?

By default, threat data is retained for 13 months to allow for year-over-year comparison. Log data is retained for 90 days. Enterprise customers can customize retention periods based on their compliance requirements.
